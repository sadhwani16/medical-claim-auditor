PM-JAY HEALTH BENEFIT PACKAGE (HBP) — OFFICIAL RULES EXCERPT
National Health Authority | Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY
Version: HBP 2022 (Effective April 2022)
Source: nha.gov.in

=========================================================
SECTION 1: GENERAL CONDITIONS FOR ALL CLAIMS
=========================================================

1.1 All claims must be submitted within 30 days of patient discharge.
1.2 Each claim must include: Discharge Summary, Lab Reports, Prescription, Consent Form, and Aadhaar verification record.
1.3 The claimed diagnosis must match the ICD-10 code registered at admission.
1.4 Any procedure not listed in the HBP is not reimbursable under PM-JAY.
1.5 A beneficiary can avail treatment up to ₹5 lakh per family per year across all packages.
1.6 ABUSE / FRAUD WARNING: Upcoding (billing for a higher-cost package than the procedure performed), phantom billing (billing for services not rendered), and length-of-stay manipulation are criminal offences under the Fraud in Healthcare scheme. Penalties include blacklisting and prosecution under IPC Section 420.

  Package Rate: ₹6,000

  Included: Surgery, local anaesthesia, eye drops for 7 days
  Excluded: Hospitalisation beyond 1 day is NOT reimbursable

Package H/02 — Squint Correction (Strabismus Surgery)
  ICD-10 Code: H49, H50
  Package Rate: ₹8,000
  Maximum Length of Stay: 1 day
  Included: Surgery, general/local anaesthesia, post-op review
  Note: Bilateral same-day squint correction is not permitted under a single package

Package H/03 — Cataract Surgery with IOL Implantation (Phacoemulsification)
  ICD-10 Code: H26.9, H25.9
  Package Rate: ₹10,000 per eye
  Maximum Length of Stay: 1 day (day-care)
  Included: Phacoemulsification, posterior chamber IOL, post-op eye drops
  NOT INCLUDED: Bilateral same-sitting surgery NOT permitted under one package
  FRAUD INDICATOR: Any claim billing more than 1 day stay for uncomplicated cataract surgery must be flagged for review. Claims above ₹12,000 per eye are anomalous.
  COMPLIANCE NOTE: Foldable IOL implantation is the standard. PMMA lens use requires documented clinical justification.

Package H/04 — Corneal Transplant (Penetrating Keratoplasty)
  ICD-10 Code: H17, H18
  Package Rate: ₹35,000
  Maximum Length of Stay: 5 days
  Included: Surgery, immunosuppressive medication for 30 days, donor cornea
  FRAUD INDICATOR: Bills exceeding ₹40,000 or stays beyond 5 days require mandatory audit.

Package H/05 — Vitreoretinal Surgery (Retinal Detachment)
  ICD-10 Code: H33.0
  Package Rate: ₹40,000
  Maximum Length of Stay: 3 days
  Included: Pars plana vitrectomy or scleral buckle, silicon oil if needed

SECTION 8: ANTI-FRAUD GUIDELINES
=========================================================

8.1 UPCODING: Submitting a claim under a higher-value package than the actual procedure performed. E.g., billing laparoscopic cholecystectomy (D/07, ₹25,000) for a patient who only received an OPD consultation. Penalty: blacklisting + full clawback + 2× penalty on amount.

8.2 LENGTH-OF-STAY MANIPULATION: Admitting a patient for longer than medically necessary to inflate per-diem charges. Auditors must verify nursing notes, daily vitals, and attending physician notes match the claimed stay duration.

8.3 PHANTOM PROCEDURES: Billing for procedures that were not performed. Mandatory cross-check: operation theatre log, anaesthesia record, and implant stickers (if applicable) are required for all surgical packages.

8.4 DUPLICATE BILLING: Submitting the same claim to PM-JAY and another insurance scheme (CGHS, ESIS) simultaneously. Real-time Claim Identity Resolver (CIR) checks must be run before payment.

8.5 GHOST PATIENTS: Billing for patients who were not admitted or do not exist. Hospital must provide: Aadhaar-verified e-token, biometric OTP at admission, and discharge biometric confirmation.

8.6 UNNECESSARY PROCEDURES: Performing and billing for procedures that are clinically unjustifiable for the stated diagnosis. E.g., performing a coronary angiography on a 25-year-old admitted for a minor orthopaedic injury with no cardiac risk factors.
